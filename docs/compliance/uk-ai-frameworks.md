# UK AI Frameworks — Alignment Statement

This document maps the PDA Platform to four UK government AI and data frameworks. It is intended to support procurement, audit, and governance teams who need to understand how the platform addresses responsible AI principles in a project delivery context.

These are self-assessed alignment statements. PDA Platform is not a certified compliance tool. Alignment does not imply formal accreditation against any framework.

---

## 1. AI Playbook for the UK Government (2024)

The AI Playbook sets out how government organisations should procure, deploy, and govern AI systems. Its principal themes are transparency, human oversight, security, and value for money.

### Transparency

The playbook requires that AI systems used in government be explainable and that their outputs not be treated as authoritative without human review.

PDA Platform addresses this through three mechanisms. First, the entire codebase is published under the MIT licence at [github.com/antnewman/pda-platform](https://github.com/antnewman/pda-platform). There are no proprietary algorithms; any practitioner or auditor can inspect the logic for every tool. Second, every AI-generated output includes a confidence score on a 0.0–1.0 scale, derived from multi-sample consensus in the `agent-task-planning` package. Third, all 58 MCP tools are documented with explicit input/output schemas in `specs/mcp-servers/`.

### Human Oversight

The playbook states that humans must remain in control of consequential decisions. PDA Platform is designed exclusively as an advisory system. All 58 tools produce recommendations, assessments, or scored outputs — none of them trigger automated decisions or actions. The Gate Readiness Assessor (P14) synthesises P1–P13 signals into a composite score and a prioritised list of recommendations, but the gate decision itself remains with the human review panel. The Override Decision Logger (P6) records when governance decisions proceed against assurance advice, creating an auditable trail without preventing the override.

### Security and Data Minimisation

The playbook requires that AI systems handle government data with appropriate controls. PDA Platform processes project schedule, cost, risk, and compliance data only — no personal data and no named individuals. API credentials are stored as environment variables and are never written to the AssuranceStore.

## 2. Data and AI Ethics Framework (CDDO, 2022)

The CDDO framework defines six principles for ethical AI and data use in government: transparency, accountability, fairness, safety, security, and contestability.

### Transparency

The framework requires that AI systems be open about how they work and what data they use. PDA Platform's open-source licence ensures full code visibility. Confidence scores accompany every AI extraction so that practitioners can see not just what the system concluded but how strongly. The `track_review_actions` tool (P3) returns both extracted actions and a confidence value for each extraction, enabling practitioners to prioritise human review of low-confidence outputs.

### Accountability

The framework requires clear audit trails for AI-influenced decisions. The AssuranceStore (a SQLite database at `~/.pm_data_tools/store.db` by default) persists every AI-generated recommendation with a timestamp, the tool name that produced it, the confidence score, and the project identifier. No record is overwritten; the store accumulates a full history. P6 (Override Decision Logger) adds a layer of explicit accountability by recording the rationale, decision maker, and outcome whenever a governance decision overrides an assurance recommendation.

### Fairness

The framework requires that AI systems not introduce bias based on protected characteristics or irrelevant personal attributes. PDA Platform processes no personal, demographic, or sensitive personal data. Inputs are project identifiers, schedule dates, cost figures, compliance scores, and unstructured review text. There is no mechanism by which the system could make decisions on the basis of individual characteristics.

### Safety

The framework requires that AI systems operate within documented limitations and not cause harm through overconfidence. The P4 Confidence Divergence Monitor actively detects when AI extraction samples diverge, when consensus scores are low, or when confidence is declining across review cycles, and raises a signal before any output is acted upon. All tools document their limitations in the technical specification (`specs/mcp-servers/`). NISTA compliance scores generated by the validator are explicitly described as indicative assessments against the trial standard, not formal certifications.

### Contestability

The framework requires that decisions influenced by AI can be challenged and corrected. Because the AssuranceStore records a full history and P6 logs overrides, any practitioner can inspect past outputs, contest a recommendation, and record that contestation. The store schema is documented in `docs/database-schema.md`, enabling independent audit.

## 3. Model for Responsible Innovation (IPA/Cabinet Office, 2023)

The Model for Responsible Innovation was developed for major government programmes. It addresses how novel approaches — including AI — should be introduced into delivery without compromising programme outcomes or public accountability.

### Innovation Governance

The model requires that innovation be introduced through structured governance with clear evidence trails. PDA Platform is designed to integrate with existing IPA review structures. The P14 Gate Readiness Assessor maps directly to the seven IPA review points (Gate 0 through Gate 5 and Post-Award Review), applying gate-specific dimension weights to produce a composite readiness score. Gate assessments are stored in the AssuranceStore, providing the evidence trail that the model requires.

### Risk Management

The model requires that innovation risk be identified and managed explicitly. P11 (Assumption Drift Tracker) registers programme assumptions with baselines and detects drift, including cascade impact analysis showing which dependent risks, benefits, and decisions are affected when an assumption shifts. P13 (Benefits Realisation Management) provides IPA/Green Book-compliant benefit registers with drift detection and realisation forecasting.

### Proportionality

The model requires that the assurance burden be proportionate to the complexity and risk profile of the programme. P5 (Adaptive Review Scheduler) and P8 (Assurance Overhead Optimiser) address this directly. P5 recommends review timing based on actual P1–P4 signals rather than fixed calendar intervals. P8 tracks assurance effort, correlates it with confidence outcomes, and identifies waste — duplicate checks, zero-finding reviews, and misallocated effort.

### Learning and Improvement

The model requires that lessons from innovation be captured and shared. P7 (Lessons Learned Knowledge Engine) ingests structured lessons from project history and provides keyword and semantic search so that relevant lessons surface at the point of decision-making rather than being buried in post-project reports.

## 4. NISTA Programme and Project Data Standard

The NISTA Standard defines the data model, reporting fields, and submission format for major government programmes. PDA Platform is built specifically to support the NISTA trial.

### Data Standard Implementation

The `pm-data-tools` package implements a NISTA parser that ingests data in the NISTA format, a NISTA validator that checks compliance against the standard's field requirements, and a NISTA exporter that produces conformant output for submission. The NISTA validator returns a compliance score (0–100) with a breakdown of failing fields and the reasons for failure.

### Longitudinal Reporting

The NISTA Standard requires consistent reporting across quarters so that trends are comparable. P2 (Longitudinal Compliance Tracker) persists compliance scores over time, detects trend direction (IMPROVING / STAGNATING / DEGRADING), and raises alerts when scores breach configured thresholds. The `pm-nista` module supports GMPP quarterly reporting and can generate AI-written DCA narratives via the `generate_narrative` tool.

### Sandbox Mode

For teams evaluating NISTA compliance without submitting to the live NISTA API, the platform supports a sandbox mode that validates data locally against the standard schema without making external API calls.

## What PDA Platform Does Not Claim

PDA Platform is not a certified compliance tool. The alignment statements above are self-assessed by the platform maintainer against published framework documentation. They have not been independently verified by CDDO, the IPA, or any other government body.

NISTA compliance scores produced by the validator are indicative assessments against the trial standard. They do not constitute formal certification and should not be presented as such in official programme returns without independent review.
