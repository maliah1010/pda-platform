## Day 3: The Story (The Lesson)

**Format:** Text post with image (no video, no carousel — pure storytelling)

### Post Copy

```
Two rail electrification programmes.
Same inflation shock. Completely different outcomes.

Programme A linked its cost assumptions to live ONS data
at the outset. When construction materials inflation hit 73%
above baseline, the system flagged it automatically. The SRO
demonstrated the exact cost and schedule impact to HMT
within 48 hours. Supplementary funding arrived in 6 weeks.
No delay.

Programme B treated assumptions as static text in a business
case. Nobody checked. By the time the overrun surfaced,
it was £120m over budget and 14 months late. The SRO was
replaced.

The difference wasn't intelligence, budget, or risk appetite.

It was whether assumption management was treated as passive
documentation or active assurance.

That's what OPAL is built to do. Not replace human judgement —
augment it with continuous, data-driven assurance signals.

12 modules. 251 maturity criteria. 29 MCP tools.
Open source. Free to use. Right now.

The paper: doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18711384
The platform: github.com/antnewman/pda-platform
Try it: pda-platform-i33p.onrender.com/sse

From policy to practice.

#OPAL #ProjectManagement #LessonsLearned #GovTech
```
